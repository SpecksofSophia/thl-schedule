What You Need to Know

What is a P&I Account?

Each resident has their own money account called Personal & Incidentals (P&I). This money is THEIRS, not the facility's. We manage it, but it must always be kept completely separate from facility funds.

Commingling means mixing resident money with facility money. This is a licensing violation under Title 22 §87219.
  • Using a resident's money to buy supplies for the facility = commingling
  • Depositing resident funds into a facility bank account = commingling
  • Keeping one cash box for both facility and resident money = commingling

Financial Abuse Awareness

Even minor or perceived financial abuse is taken seriously. Staff are mandated reporters.

  • Borrowing money from a resident, even with intent to return it
  • Making purchases without the resident's knowledge or consent
  • Not returning change from a purchase
  • Keeping receipts vague or incomplete
  • Using a resident's account for unauthorized items
QA looks for patterns. Consistent, accurate documentation protects everyone -- the resident AND you.

Quick Check

3 Questions
Q1. A staff member uses $15 from Bonnie's P&I account to buy cleaning supplies for the facility kitchen. Is this allowed?
AYes, as long as it's under $20
BYes, if you write it down
CNo, that's commingling -- resident money can only be used for the resident
DOnly if the administrator approves it
Resident funds are strictly for the resident's personal use. Using them for facility expenses is commingling, a violation under Title 22 §87219. Even with a receipt, this is not allowed.
Q2. What is needed when a resident requests a cash withdrawal?
AJust tell Anthony about it later
BThe amount, what it's for, and the resident's signature
CNothing if it's under $20
DA verbal okay from the resident
Every cash withdrawal needs: the amount, a description of what it's for, and the resident's signature. The bot generates a signature link on your phone.

CPhoto it and send to @PILedgersBot with the resident's name

Every receipt gets photographed and sent to @PILedgersBot. The bot links it to the transaction automatically. This creates the audit trail that protects everyone.
