The 10 Reportable Incidents

If you see any of these, it needs a report. No exceptions.

ER Visit
Any time a resident goes to the emergency room, for any reason
QA Focus
Fall
Any fall, whether or not there's visible injury. Include where and how it happened
Seizure
Any seizure activity. Note how long it lasted
Choking
Any choking incident during meals or otherwise
QA Focus
Medication Error
Wrong dose, wrong med, wrong time, or missed dose
Injury Needing Care
Cuts, burns, bruises that need medical attention
Abuse or Neglect Allegation
Any allegation of physical, verbal, or emotional abuse or neglect, even if suspected
Death
Any death at the facility or while in care
Missing Person
Can't find a resident or they left the facility without permission
Law Enforcement
Police called to or visiting the facility for any reason

Quick Check

3 Questions

Make sure it's clear before you go

Question 1
A resident trips on the rug and falls. They say they're fine and there are no visible injuries. Do you report it?
Any fall gets reported. Even if there's no injury. QA will ask about this.
Question 2
How quickly must an incident be reported?
Title 22 section 80061 requires reporting within 24 hours or next business day.
Question 3
Which of these does NOT require a Special Incident Report?
Refusing a meal is a personal choice, not an incident. The other three are all reportable.
